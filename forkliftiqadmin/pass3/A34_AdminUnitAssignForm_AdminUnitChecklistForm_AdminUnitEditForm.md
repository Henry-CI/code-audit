### A34-6 [MEDIUM] — AdminUnitEditForm: `validate()` undocumented non-trivial public method
**File:** `AdminUnitEditForm.java`, lines 64–108
**Detail:** `validate(ActionMapping, HttpServletRequest)` is a non-trivial public method with significant logic: it enforces required fields (`name`, `serial_no`, `manu_id`, `type_id`, `fuel_type_id`), applies a regex pattern to `size`, constructs a `UnitBean`, and sets a request attribute `"arrAdminUnit"` as a side effect. None of this behavior is documented. The side effect of populating `request` attribute `arrAdminUnit` inside a validation method is especially noteworthy and undocumented.
**Missing:** Class-level and method-level Javadoc; no `@param` for `mapping` or `request`; no `@return` describing the `ActionErrors` result.

---

### A34-7 [MEDIUM] — AdminUnitEditForm: `getUnit(String)` undocumented non-trivial public method
**File:** `AdminUnitEditForm.java`, lines 110–133
**Detail:** `getUnit(String compId)` is a non-trivial public method that builds and returns a `UnitBean` from all form fields, with several conditional conversions (blank `id` becomes `null`; blank `size`/`hourmeter` default to `0.0d`; blank `keypad_reader` becomes `null` vs. an enum conversion). The `compId` parameter is injected into `comp_id` on the resulting bean — callers must understand this to use the method correctly. No Javadoc exists.
**Missing:** `@param compId`, `@return` describing the built `UnitBean` and its field-mapping behavior.

---

### A34-8 [MEDIUM] — AdminUnitEditForm: `setUnit(UnitBean)` undocumented non-trivial public method
**File:** `AdminUnitEditForm.java`, lines 136–142
**Detail:** `setUnit(UnitBean)` uses reflection via `PropertyUtils.copyProperties()` to bulk-copy properties from a `UnitBean` into this form. This is non-trivial behavior (reflective bulk copy, exception wrapping into `RuntimeException`). No Javadoc documents the purpose, expected input, or the `RuntimeException` that may be thrown if property copying fails.
**Missing:** `@param unitBean`, description of the reflective copy mechanism, and note about `RuntimeException` on failure.

---

### A34-9 [LOW] — AdminUnitEditForm: Constructor `AdminUnitEditForm()` undocumented
**File:** `AdminUnitEditForm.java`, lines 50–54
**Detail:** The public constructor has no Javadoc. It initializes `arrAdminUnitType` and `arrAdminUnitFuelType` via DAO calls, which means construction has I/O side effects and throws a checked `Exception`. Callers are not warned about this. This is a getter/setter-equivalent in structure but carries meaningful side effects; borderline MEDIUM but recorded as LOW given the constructor's primary role is initialization.

### A34-10 [LOW] — AdminUnitEditForm: Lombok-generated accessors for `arrMenufacture` field have a typo
**File:** `AdminUnitEditForm.java`, line 37
**Detail:** The field is named `arrMenufacture` (misspelling of "Manufacturer"). Lombok will generate `getArrMenufacture()` / `setArrMenufacture()` from this name, propagating the misspelling into the public API. This is not a Javadoc issue per se, but it is a documentation/naming quality issue that makes generated method names misleading. No Javadoc is present to clarify intent.
